Publishers, brands, technology providers, and policymakers uniting to establish transparency, attribution, and fair compensation when AI systems use proprietary content.

Collective Bargaining: Content creators facing AI's extraction economy alone get one-off deals and declining traffic. Together, we negotiate from strength - building technical tools, shaping policy, and establishing compensation frameworks that work.

Our Five Goals

OpenAttribution pursues five goals to ensure content creators can track, verify, and monetize their work when used by AI systems.

  1. 1. Transparency

    Content owners know when and where their information is used - consumers understand the sources

  2. 2. Attribution & Credit

    Proper credit when content informs AI outputs, enabling tracking and verification

  3. 3. Accuracy

    Faithful representation of product details, brand messaging, and published content

  4. 4. Fair Compensation

    Appropriate compensation when proprietary information drives value

  5. 5. Economic Viability

    Ensure content creation remains economically viable while AI has quality data

The Problem

AI Systems Are Consuming Content Without Attribution

AI systems from ChatGPT to Claude to Gemini are consuming publisher content at massive scale while providing zero visibility into usage. Major publishers report 20-40% traffic declines as AI-powered search and chatbots answer questions directly without sending users to source websites.

Scale Without Precedent

Decades of human knowledge consumed instantly, monetized immediately - breaking every framework we've developed for fair use and compensation

Reformulation Without Attribution

AI synthesizes and presents content without users knowing the sources, biases, or reliability of the information

Terminal Conversations

Designed by behavioral psychologists to maximize engagement, AI chats keep users from ever reaching content sources. No pageview. No ad impression. No subscription. Just extraction.

Quality Degradation Risk

Without attribution and compensation, quality content creators stop creating. AI systems, which cannot innovate or judge quality themselves, become increasingly mediocre.

The Internet's Economic Bargain Is Breaking

Search traffic that powered the web for decades is collapsing. Health sites down 10%. News sites seeing 56% to 69% of searches result in no clicks. As one CEO puts it: "AI is basically choking off traffic to most content sites."

Frequently Asked Questions

How is AI content consumption different from human learning?

The difference is scale, velocity, and transparency. Humans gradually absorb knowledge over years, show their credentials and biases, and compensate teachers. AI consumes billions of articles instantly, operates as a black box, and generates immediate revenue without compensating creators. Our existing frameworks were built for human-scale learning, not industrial-scale extraction.

Won't attribution slow down AI innovation?

Sustainable growth beats extractive growth. Without attribution and fair compensation, quality content creators withdraw. AI systems, which cannot judge quality or create genuinely new ideas, become increasingly unreliable. Attribution ensures the content ecosystem that makes AI valuable continues to thrive.

How is ACAS different from robots.txt?

Robots.txt is binary - block or allow. ACAS enables measured access with attribution. Content owners can allow AI usage while tracking it, enabling various business models from licensing to revenue sharing. It's not about blocking AI, but creating transparency that enables fair value exchange.

What's the incentive for AI companies to adopt ACAS?

Multiple pressures are converging: Legal (mounting fair use challenges), Technical (dependency on current content to avoid hallucinations), Economic (publishers blocking scrapers), and Competitive (alternative models emerging). Quality content makes AI valuable - attribution creates sustainable access.

Does ACAS track user data?

No. ACAS is privacy-first and does not collect any personal information. It only tracks content usage patterns and attribution signals between AI systems and content sources. Users benefit from transparency without surveillance.

How quickly can ACAS be implemented?

RAG attribution can be implemented in days - AI systems already know what content they retrieve at query time. Full implementation is incremental: start with real-time retrieval, add response attribution, then expand to training transparency as technical solutions mature.

How does ACAS relate to standards like C2PA?

ACAS follows the successful coalition model of standards like C2PA, which brought together 500+ companies to establish content provenance. While C2PA focuses on media file authenticity (images, videos), ACAS addresses AI chat attribution. We're learning from C2PA's approach: industry collaboration, cryptographic verification, and incremental adoption.
