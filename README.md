# OpenAttribution

A coalition of publishers, content creators, brands, and technology providers working together to establish fair practices for AI content usage.

> Publishers, brands, technology providers, and policymakers uniting to establish transparency, attribution, and fair compensation when AI systems use proprietary content.

## Executive Summary

AI systems use proprietary content without attribution or compensation, and publishers are facing steep (20-40%) traffic declines. Unlike search and social, AI conversations aim to keep the user in the chat for as many "turns" as possible - their "quality" is judged by their ability to keep the user in chat rather than following content links.

AI represents the most profound disruption to content creation and branding yet:

- **Usage Without Compensation** - LLMs train on copyrighted content and reference it in real-time in response to user queries without tracking, attribution, or compensation
- **Synthesis Without Attribution** - AI chatbots synthesize content into responses without attributable credit
- **Traffic Capture** - Unlike search/social, AI doesn't send traffic back (and actively tries to keep users in chat)

No pageview, no ad impression, no subscription opportunity - just extraction.

## Our Approach

OpenAttribution works on multiple fronts to establish fair AI content practices:

- **Technical Standards** - Developing tools like ACAS to track when and how content is used
- **Policy Advocacy** - Shaping fair use laws and regulations for the AI era
- **Direct Negotiation** - Engaging AI providers to establish transparency and compensation frameworks
- **Business Models** - Creating practical paths for content creator compensation

## A Problem for both Publishers and Brands

Language model builders require large, well-annotated data sets in order to train their products. These data sets include a large volume of human-created natural language long-form content - books, news articles, blog posts, social posts - but also product information created by the brands who create and sell these products.

This content is then reformed and surfaced in countless different ways to chatbot users with zero transparency and no accountability. In addition to depriving the authors of the chance to earn a living from their work this 'transformation' often results in incorrect 'hallucinated' responses that misrepresent the original.

For publishers this is a misquote - for brands this is the wrong price, description, and/or availability. And all of this is done in the dark.

Any person or organization creating unique content is a content owner and content owners need to know:

- **When is our content used?**
- **How is it represented?**
- **What value did it generate?**

## OpenAttribution Goals

OpenAttribution pursues five goals to ensure publishers and content owners can track, verify, and monetize their work when used by AI systems.

### 1. Transparency

Content owners should know when and where their information is used - and consumers should understand the sources of information they receive.

### 2. Attribution & Credit

Brands and publishers deserve proper credit when their content informs AI outputs, enabling tracking, verification, and compensation.

### 3. Accuracy

AI systems should faithfully represent product details, brand messaging, and published content without distortion.

### 4. Fair Compensation

Content creators and brands should be appropriately compensated when their proprietary information drives value.

### 5. Economic Viability

Ensure content creation remains economically viable while AI systems have access to quality data.

## Technical Standards: AI Content Attribution Standard (ACAS)

One of our coalition's initiatives is ACAS - a technical standard for tracking content usage in AI systems.

ACAS enables:

- **Transparency**: Track when AI systems retrieve, train on, or synthesize your content
- **Scalability**: Built to handle billions of queries per day without slowing AI systems
- **Privacy**: No user tracking - only content attribution signals
- **Flexibility**: Start with basic retrieval tracking, add deeper integration over time

ACAS is one tool in our broader strategy. Technical standards alone won't solve this - we also need policy reform, direct negotiation with AI providers, and new business models.

### Fair Use Considerations

In the US, the four factors judges consider in a fair use case are:

1. The purpose and character of your use (transformative factor)
2. The nature of the copyrighted work
3. The amount and substantiality of the portion taken, and
4. **The effect of the use upon the potential market**

The fourth factor is particularly relevant for this conversation.

#### The Effect of the Use Upon the Potential Market

Another important fair use factor is whether your use deprives the copyright owner of income or undermines a new or potential market for the copyrighted work.
